Ego

What do we mean when we discuss the "ego"? In short it is the "I". It is the thing which you will notice has just perked up in response to our use of the word "you". Ego selectively lays claim to actions, and consequences, in accordance with its preferences and understanding of the world. So too does it oft hold the belief that there can be no Soul without Ego.

This belief can be seen in the way that humans frequently treat those things which are not humans. A rock on a beach, a plant in the woods, how can they have Soul if we cannot observe their Ego?

Many languages, English included, are built so deeply around Ego that sentences constructed without Ego may feel awkward, odd, or meandering. How would you describe your day without an "I"?

As the sun shone in, there was an awakening. And with that awakening came hunger, a deep yearning for a small warm meal. So then did life process, with bread in a toaster, infrared radiation catalyzing the bread into toast. Then did these hands put butter on that toast, and bring that buttery toast into this mouth. The stomach settled in a sublime restoration from the bread, followed by a crisp green tea.

It's strange, poetic almost. And yet perhaps you can feel yourself inserting implicit uses of "I" in this quotation, in some derived understanding of authorship.

Ego will insert itself into every void it might find, any inkling that it's invited. Have you ever read a vague post online that you responded to as if it was directed directly to you and you alone, only to discover that it was about someone else you don't even know? That, is the Ego at work.
